The boy looks around at his friends and they all nod their heads at him before he turns his sights back at you. “Well, listen here Verner, you come here uninvited, and us lads don’t take too kindly when outsiders show up the way you just did,” he continues saying just as the rest of the boys start to encircle you. “If you want me to leave, just say so,” you reply. “Ah, too late for that I’m afraid,” he says, wiggling his finger at you. “Tsk, tsk, tsk, if we let you leave just like that, you’ll just go and ruin someone else's playground, thinking it’s all nice and dandy – and if they then find out I let you think it was fine – I will be the one they blame!”
