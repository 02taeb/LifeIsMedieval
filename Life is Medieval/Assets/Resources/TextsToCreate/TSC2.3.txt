“They say death’s breath is as cold as ice,” your father tells you, as you both stand peering out through the window watching the first snowflakes fall to the ground. “Us commoners know that too well, my son. Look,” he says, and points to some of the older villagers working the fields. “You won’t see them there for much longer, soon they'll succumb to death’s icy breath.” He says, and gives you a pat on your back. “That’s why you need to start thinking of what you want to become when you grow older, do you want to be a poor commoner like your father and like the workers on the field? Toiling away, always hungry and cold, till one day when you finally die?”
